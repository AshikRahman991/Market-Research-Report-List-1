<p><p>The energy-efficient windows market includes various types designed to minimize heat transfer and enhance insulation. Double glazing involves two panes of glass with a gas-filled space in between, providing good thermal performance and reducing noise. Triple glazing adds an additional pane, offering even better energy efficiency, ideal for extreme climates. Low-E (low emissivity) coatings are applied to glass surfaces to reflect heat while allowing light in, significantly improving energy conservation. Together, these technologies cater to rising energy efficiency demands in residential and commercial buildings.</p></p>
