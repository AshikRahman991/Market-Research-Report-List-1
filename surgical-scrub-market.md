<p><p>The surgical scrub market is primarily divided into two types: waterless and water-based scrubs. Waterless surgical scrubs, often gel or foam-based, offer convenience and quick application, effectively reducing microbial presence without the need for rinsing. They are ideal in settings where water availability is limited. In contrast, water-based scrubs require rinsing and typically contain soaps or antiseptics that provide thorough cleansing of the skin. Both types aim to enhance hand hygiene and prevent surgical site infections in healthcare environments.</p></p>
