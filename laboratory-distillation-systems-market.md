<p><p>Laboratory distillation systems encompass various market types based on the distillation technique employed. Distillation involves separating liquid mixtures by utilizing differences in boiling points. Steam distillation enhances the process using steam to extract essential oils or heat-sensitive compounds. Vacuum distillation operates under reduced pressure to lower boiling points, ideal for thermally sensitive materials. Fractional distillation employs a column to separate components with close boiling points, making it effective for purifying complex mixtures. Each method serves distinct applications in research, pharmaceuticals, and chemical processing.</p></p>
