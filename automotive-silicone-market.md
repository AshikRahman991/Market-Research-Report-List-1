<p><ul><li>High Temperature Vulcanised (HTV)</li><li>Room Temperature Vulcanised (RTV)</li><li>Liquid Silicone Rubber (LSR)</li><li>Fluorosilicone Rubber (FSR)</li><li>High Consistency Silicone Rubber (HCR)</li><li>Silicone Gels</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive silicone market comprises several types, each serving specific applications. High Temperature Vulcanised (HTV) silicone offers durability in extreme heat, while Room Temperature Vulcanised (RTV) silicone is flexible and easy to apply at ambient temperatures. Liquid Silicone Rubber (LSR) is known for its high precision molding capabilities. Fluorosilicone Rubber (FSR) provides chemical resistance for harsh environments. High Consistency Silicone Rubber (HCR) delivers robust performance in demanding conditions, and silicone gels offer cushioning and sealing properties in various automotive components.</p></p>
