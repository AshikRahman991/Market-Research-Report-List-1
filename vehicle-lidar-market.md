<p><p>Vehicle LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging) is a remote sensing technology that uses laser pulses to measure distances and create high-resolution 3D maps of the surrounding environment. Additionally, there is a growing focus on miniaturization and energy efficiency to meet the requirements of electric and autonomous vehicles. The integration of artificial intelligence (AI) with LiDAR systems is another emerging trend, enabling real-time data processing and decision-making. As the automotive industry continues to evolve, Vehicle LiDAR is poised to play a pivotal role in shaping the future of mobility.</p></p>

<p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Vehicle LiDAR Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Mechanical Lidar</li><li>Solid State Lidar</li><li>Hybrid Solid-state Lidar</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Vehicle LiDAR market comprises three main types: Mechanical LiDAR uses rotating components to emit and receive laser beams, offering wide field-of-view but higher costs and fragility. Solid-State LiDAR relies on stationary components, enhancing durability and compactness, though with a narrower field-of-view. Hybrid Solid-State LiDAR combines elements of both, using micro-mirrors or other semi-moving parts to balance performance, durability, and cost. Each type caters to varying automotive needs, from autonomous driving to advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS).</p></p>
