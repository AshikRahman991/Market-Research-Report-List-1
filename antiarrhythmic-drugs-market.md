<p><p>The antiarrhythmic drugs market is categorized into two main types: oral antiarrhythmic and intravenous antiarrhythmic agents. Oral antiarrhythmic medications are taken by mouth and are typically used for long-term management of various cardiac arrhythmias, offering convenience and ease of administration. In contrast, intravenous antiarrhythmic drugs are administered directly into the bloodstream, providing rapid therapeutic effects, often employed in acute settings for immediate arrhythmia control. Both types are crucial in maintaining cardiac rhythm stability and improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
