<p><p>The intraocular lens (IOL) material market comprises four primary types: </p><p>1. **Polymethyl Methacrylate (PMMA)**: A hard, durable lens known for its stability and long-term performance but lacks flexibility.</p><p>2. **Silicone**: Offers flexibility and is less prone to distortion, providing comfort during implantation.</p><p>3. **Hydrophilic Acrylic or Hydrogel**: Absorbs water, resulting in a softer lens that adapts well to the eye, reducing dryness.</p><p>4. **Hydrophobic Acrylic**: Features water-resistant properties, promoting better optical quality and minimizing protein buildup, enhancing biocompatibility.</p></p>
