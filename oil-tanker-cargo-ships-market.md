<p><p>The oil tanker cargo ship market consists mainly of Very Large Crude Carriers (VLCCs) and Ultra Large Crude Carriers (ULCCs). VLCCs typically have a capacity of 200,000 to 320,000 deadweight tons, suitable for long-haul crude oil transport. ULCCs, on the other hand, exceed 320,000 deadweight tons, making them the largest tankers, ideal for transporting massive quantities of oil over vast distances. Both types are essential for global oil trade, shaping shipping routes and influencing market dynamics.</p></p>
