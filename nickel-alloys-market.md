<p><p>Nickel alloys are categorized into corrosion-resistant, heat-resistant, and high-performance markets based on their applications. Corrosion-resistant alloys excel in harsh environments, resisting degradation from chemicals, seawater, and acids, making them ideal for marine, chemical, and oil industries. Heat-resistant alloys maintain strength and stability at high temperatures, suitable for aerospace, power generation, and industrial furnaces. High-performance alloys combine both properties, offering exceptional durability, strength, and resistance to extreme conditions, often used in advanced engineering, nuclear, and defense sectors. Each type addresses specific industrial demands.</p></p>
