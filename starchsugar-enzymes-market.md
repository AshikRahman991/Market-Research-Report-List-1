<p><p>The starch-sugar enzyme market comprises various types, including alpha amylase and beta amylase. Alpha amylase is an enzyme that breaks down starch into simpler sugars during the initial stages of digestion, making it essential for food and beverage industries. Beta amylase further hydrolyzes these sugars into maltose and glucose, vital for brewing and baking. Other enzymes in this market may include glucoamylase and pullulanase, contributing to starch liquefaction and saccharification processes across various applications.</p></p>
