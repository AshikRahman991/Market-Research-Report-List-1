<p><ul><li>Classic Trainers</li><li>Smart Trainers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The bike trainers market is primarily divided into two types: classic trainers and smart trainers. Classic trainers are traditional, resistance-based devices that allow cyclists to ride stationary bikes, offering basic functionality without connectivity. In contrast, smart trainers feature advanced technology, including digital resistance control, integration with cycling apps, and real-time performance tracking. This makes smart trainers appealing for those seeking a more interactive and immersive cycling experience, often enhancing workouts through simulated courses and competition features. Both options cater to different cycling needs.</p></p>
