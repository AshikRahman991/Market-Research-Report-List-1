<p><p>The Non-invasive Brain Stimulation System market comprises primarily two types: Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS) and Transcranial Current Stimulation (tCS). TMS utilizes magnetic fields to stimulate nerve cells in the brain, primarily for treating depression and other neuropsychiatric disorders. In contrast, tCS employs weak electrical currents to modulate neuronal activity, aiding in cognitive enhancement and rehabilitation. Both systems are gaining traction for their therapeutic potential without requiring surgical interventions, appealing to healthcare providers and researchers alike.</p></p>
