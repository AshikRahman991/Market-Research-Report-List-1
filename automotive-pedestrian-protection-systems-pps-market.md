<p><p>Automotive Pedestrian Protection Systems (PPS) are designed to enhance the safety of pedestrians during vehicle collisions. They are categorized into two main types: Passive and Active Pedestrian Protection Systems. Passive systems include features like crumple zones and energy-absorbing bumpers that reduce impact forces. In contrast, Active systems leverage advanced technologies such as sensors and cameras to detect pedestrians and initiate preventive measures, such as automatic braking or steering adjustments, thereby actively preventing or mitigating accidents.</p></p>
