<p><ul><li>Internal Shaft Locking Devices</li><li>External Shaft Locking Devices</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The shaft locking devices market is divided into two primary types: internal and external shaft locking devices. Internal shaft locking devices are integrated within the shaft system, utilizing mechanisms like set screws or keyways to prevent rotation and maintain position. In contrast, external shaft locking devices are mounted externally, offering versatile solutions for securing shafts with clamps, collars, or other attachments. Both types provide reliability and safety in various industrial applications, ensuring effective operation in rotating machinery and equipment.</p></p>
