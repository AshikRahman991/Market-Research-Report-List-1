<p><p>The rail car wheel market is primarily divided into two types: rolling steel wheels and cast steel wheels. Rolling steel wheels are produced through a process of rolling and forging, offering superior strength and durability, making them ideal for high-performance applications. In contrast, cast steel wheels are created by pouring molten steel into molds, providing cost-effectiveness and good manufacturing flexibility. Both types serve critical roles in railway operations, with specific advantages catering to diverse operational needs and rail environments.</p></p>
