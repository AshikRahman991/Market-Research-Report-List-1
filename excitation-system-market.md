<p><p>Excitation systems in power markets can be categorized into analog and digital types based on their control mechanisms. Analog excitation systems utilize continuous signals for regulating voltage and reactive power, offering simplicity and reliability. In contrast, digital excitation systems employ microprocessors and software algorithms for enhanced control, enabling complex features like automatic tuning and real-time monitoring. Both systems aim to ensure stable power generation and grid stability, yet digital systems provide greater flexibility and integration with modern control architectures.</p></p>
