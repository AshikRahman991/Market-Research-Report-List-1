<p><p>The satellite phone market is primarily divided into two types: geosynchronous satellite phones and low-earth orbit (LEO) satellite phones. Geosynchronous satellite phones rely on satellites positioned 22,236 miles above Earth, providing consistent coverage over a fixed area, ideal for remote communication. In contrast, LEO satellite phones connect through satellites orbiting at lower altitudes, enhancing signal quality and reducing latency, while requiring a larger constellation of satellites for global coverage. Both serve critical roles in communication for various industries.</p></p>
