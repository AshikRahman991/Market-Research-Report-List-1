<p><p>Interactive Voice Response (IVR) systems are categorized into two main market types: speech-based and touch-tone based. Speech-based IVR systems allow users to interact by speaking commands, providing a more natural and intuitive experience. In contrast, touch-tone based IVR systems require users to respond using a telephone keypad, often navigating through options with number inputs. Both types enhance customer engagement and streamline service delivery, but differ in user interaction methods, catering to varying preferences and technological capabilities.</p></p>
