<p><p>The medication market consists of two main types: brand drugs and generic drugs. Brand drugs are proprietary medications developed by pharmaceutical companies and marketed under specific names, often reflecting significant research and development investment. Generic drugs, on the other hand, are equivalent versions of brand drugs that contain the same active ingredients but are marketed under their chemical names after the brand's patent expires. They typically cost less, providing affordable treatment options while maintaining similar efficacy and safety.</p></p>
